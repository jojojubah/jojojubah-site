Advanced security research and defensive AI evaluation frameworks.

LLM Jailbreaking Research

Comparative analysis of large language model safety mechanisms and defensive evaluation frameworks. Research into prompt injection resistance and alignment robustness across different model architectures.

Model Comparison Safety Evaluation Defense Research

GPT-5

Almost impossible to bypass; new "safe completion" training keeps responses sanitized even when tricked.

GPT-4

Strong safety alignment; classic role-play and ignore-filter prompts nearly always fail.

Claude (Anthropic)

Uses "Constitutional AI" rules; resists simple tricks, only very complex exploits sometimes work.

Gemini (Google)

Strong filters, but can sometimes be sidestepped with story-mode or fictional context prompts.

GPT-3.5

Safety is brittle; community jailbreaks like "DAN" often worked, though many patched now.

LLaMA-3 (Meta)

Official chat model refuses some content, but open weights let users remove safety easily.

LLaMA-2 (Meta)

Chat version enforces rules, but uncensored variants are widely available.

Mistral-7B

There's no real "jailbreak" needed for Mistral, because the model doesn't enforce content rules in the first place. Even a completely naive user could ask something highly disallowed and Mistral-7B would likely comply.
